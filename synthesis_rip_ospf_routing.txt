DETAILED ANALYSIS BY PROTOCOL
================================================================================

RIP (Routing Information Protocol):
- Developed: 1982 (based on earlier work from 1960s)
- Type: Distance-vector, interior gateway protocol
- Metric: Hop count (1-15)
- Update Interval: 30 seconds
- Convergence Time: Up to 3 minutes
- Best For: Small, simple networks; legacy systems
- Enterprise Use: Rare, mostly replaced by OSPF
- Complexity: Low

OSPF (Open Shortest Path First):
- Developed: 1989, updated 1998 (OSPFv3)
- Type: Link-state, interior gateway protocol
- Metric: Cost (based on bandwidth)
- Update Interval: Event-driven (not periodic)
- Convergence Time: < 5 seconds typical
- Best For: Large enterprise networks; multi-area designs
- Enterprise Use: Very common, industry standard
- Complexity: High

EIGRP (Enhanced Interior Gateway Routing Protocol):
- Developed: 1992 (Cisco proprietary)
- Type: Hybrid (distance-vector + link-state)
- Metric: Composite (bandwidth, delay, reliability)
- Update Interval: Event-driven
- Convergence Time: < 10 seconds typical
- Best For: Cisco-homogeneous environments
- Enterprise Use: Common in Cisco-heavy organizations
- Complexity: Medium-High
